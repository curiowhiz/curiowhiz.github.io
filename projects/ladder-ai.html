The Access vs. Fluency Gap

Most professionals have access to AI tools but remain stuck at basic usage — unable to integrate AI into their workflows, automate tasks, or apply AI fluently in their roles. There's a significant gap between knowing about AI and using it productively.

🔍

Three North Star Problems

Distilled diverse pain points into three north star problems — the Prompting Gap, the Workflow Gap, and the Learning Gap — through cross-functional user research across office workers, college graduates, and Product Managers.

⚙️

Validated Pain Points

50-70% of professionals reported overlapping pain points around AI fluency, with Product Managers showing the strongest signal for AI integration needs. Three pillars validated for prototype direction.

Solution Direction

Designed a four-pillar gamified AI fluency platform: personalized onboarding with skill diagnosis, workflow capture for automation opportunities, structured teaching with active recall, and progress tracking with streaks and peer-led sessions.

Want to dive deeper?

Cross-functional user research across multiple professional segments to identify overlapping AI fluency patterns:

  • Target Audiences: Office workers, recent college graduates, and working professionals across multiple industries
  • Methodology: Structured user interviews combined with workflow observation and pain-point mapping
  • Pain Points Identified: Tool discovery friction, over-reliance on single tools, lack of contextual outputs, low trust in AI outputs, workflow disconnect, subscription fatigue
  • Key Finding: 50-70% of users reported overlapping pain points, with Product Managers showing the strongest signal for AI integration needs
  • Personalized Onboarding: Skill diagnosis quiz to assess current AI fluency level and identify role-specific needs
  • Workflow Capture: Map daily tasks and identify automation opportunities — turn manual work into AI-augmented workflows
  • Structured Teaching: Lessons with active recall and open-source concepts to build durable prompting and workflow skills
  • Rewards & Measurement: Streaks, progress tracking, and peer-led sessions to encourage ongoing learning and prevent drop-off

Step 1 — Onboarding: A PM completes an onboarding quiz that assesses their current AI skills, identifies their role's unique workflows (research synthesis, PRD writing, competitive analysis), and benchmarks them against AI-fluent PMs.

Step 2 — Workflow Mapping: The platform helps map daily PM tasks (user interviews, roadmap updates, stakeholder reports) and surfaces the top 3 repetitive workflows ripe for AI augmentation or full automation.

Step 3 — Structured Lessons: The PM follows guided lessons on writing effective prompts, building n8n automations, and chaining tools together.

Step 4 — Progress Tracking: Streaks, level-ups, and peer-led sessions keep the PM engaged — moving them from basic AI usage to advanced fluency, with measurable outputs.
