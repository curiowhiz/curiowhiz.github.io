Groww: Cut Abandonment Levels

Reducing user abandonment in the investment app through user research, competitive analysis, and hypothesis-driven testing.

User Research Competitive Analysis Hypothesis Testing Market Research Conversion Optimization

Project Overview

This product initiative tackled a critical business challenge: reducing user abandonment in the investment onboarding and account setup flows. The project combined rigorous user research with competitive benchmarking to identify friction points and design solutions.

Objective: Reduce user abandonment rate by 40% through improved onboarding experience and feature accessibility.

Problem Statement

Groww was experiencing high abandonment rates during:

  • Account verification and KYC (Know Your Customer) process - 35% abandonment
  • First investment flow - 28% abandonment
  • Fund selection and portfolio setup - 22% abandonment
  • Payment integration - 18% abandonment

Research Methodology

User Research Approach:

  • Conducted 25+ user interviews with abandoned and active users
  • Analyzed user behavior through heatmaps and session recordings
  • Surveyed 500+ users on friction points and pain areas
  • Tracked drop-off patterns across mobile and web platforms

Competitive Research:

  • Analyzed onboarding flows of 8 competing fintech platforms
  • Benchmarked conversion rates and abandonment metrics
  • Identified best practices in KYC and verification
  • Evaluated UX patterns for payment integration

Key Findings

Finding #1 - KYC Complexity: Users found the verification process confusing with unclear instructions. Average time: 12 minutes. Users unfamiliar with financial terminology abandoned at 2x rate.
Finding #2 - Choice Paralysis: Overwhelming fund options without proper guidance led to decision fatigue. Users with more than 20 fund recommendations abandoned.
Finding #3 - Trust & Education Gap: First-time investors needed more education on investment concepts before committing funds.
Finding #4 - Mobile Friction: 60% of abandonment occurred on mobile due to small form fields and difficult document uploads.

Hypotheses & Testing

Hypothesis #1: Simplifying the KYC flow with step-by-step guidance will increase completion rate by 25%

Test: A/B tested streamlined 3-step KYC vs. previous 6-step flow
Result: ✅ 32% improvement in completion rate
Hypothesis #2: Curated fund recommendations (top 5) vs. all funds will reduce abandonment in portfolio selection

Test: Tested curated recommendations with AI-based personalization
Result: ✅ 28% reduction in abandonment, 40% increase in conversion
Hypothesis #3: Educational tooltips and micro-learning modules will improve completion in first investment flow

Test: Added contextual education features and glossary
Result: ✅ 22% improvement in completion, higher user confidence

Solutions Implemented

  • Simplified KYC: Redesigned 6-step process into 3 clear steps with progress indicator
  • Smart Fund Recommendations: AI-powered curation showing top 5 recommended funds based on investment profile
  • In-app Education: Tooltips, video tutorials, and glossary for financial terms
  • Mobile Optimization: Larger form fields, improved document upload with camera functionality
  • Progress Saving: Allow users to save progress and resume later without re-entering data
  • Security Reassurance: Clear communication of security measures and data privacy

Results & Impact

📉 Overall Abandonment Reduction: 40% decrease in overall abandonment rate
💰 Conversion Increase: 35% increase in completed first investments
⏱️ Time Reduction: Average onboarding time reduced from 18 to 8 minutes
😊 User Satisfaction: NPS improved from 32 to 54 points

Key Learnings

  • Simplicity beats features - removing 50% of steps resulted in better outcomes
  • User education is critical for financial products - context matters
  • Mobile-first design is essential for fintech apps
  • Hypothesis-driven testing validates assumptions before full implementation
  • Understanding user psychology (choice paralysis, trust) drives better product decisions
